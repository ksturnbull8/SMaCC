About SMaCC

What We Are and What We Stand For

Founded in 2014, the Scottish Maternal Critical Care Network brings together multidisciplinary teams across Scotland to improve outcomes for critically ill women throughout the peri-partum journey.

Our history

Founded on Collaboration, Driven by Data

The Scottish Maternal Critical Care (SMaCC) network was founded in 2014 by a group of likeminded obstetric anaesthetists who came together to develop a Scottish Obstetric HDU education course for midwives, under the auspices of the Scottish Multi-professional Maternal Development Programme (SMMDP), part of NHS Education Scotland.

Initial aims were to collect data on obstetric patients receiving or requiring a higher level of care, and to use that data to make the case — locally and nationally — for improved education and staffing.

The SICSAG Partnership

The Scottish Intensive Care Society Audit Group (SICSAG) was already well established, collecting and publishing data from Scotland's critical care units in an annual report. SICSAG were interested in extending their reach into high dependency units — surgical, medical and, in our case, obstetric.

Individual maternity units joined the audit as SICSAG and local conditions allowed. The earliest was Ninewells Hospital in Tayside in 2013, and the most recent was Ayrshire Maternity Unit in 2019. Currently, the 7 largest maternity units in Scotland are participating in the audit.

Data is published in the annual SICSAG report, evolving from a sole entry by the first unit in 2015 to a dedicated section for obstetrics in the 2019 report. We have since introduced obstetric-specific indicators — including whether baby is with mum in obstetric HDU and whether baby requires extra care — to inform staffing conversations both locally and nationally.

7 Maternity Units

The 7 largest maternity units in Scotland now participate in the SICSAG audit, with data published annually.

SICSAG Membership Standards

Participating units adhere to predefined minimum standards and quality indicators:

  • Daily review & written management plans
  • Twice daily ward rounds
  • Appropriate staffing & skills
  • Pharmacist access
  • Physiotherapist access
  • Night-time discharge monitoring
  • Care bundles
  • Patient & family surveys
  • Clinical governance processes

What we do

Driving the EMC/MCC message forward

SMaCC continues to drive the Emergency Maternal Care / Maternal Critical Care message forward locally. We focus on embedding data collection into our maternity units, adhering to SICSAG quality indicators and minimum standards, and ensuring our midwives are appropriately trained.

Nationally, we run educational multidisciplinary symposia aimed at the whole team involved in the care of the critically ill pregnant and recently pregnant patient — showcasing Scottish expertise, bringing in outside experts, and promoting collaboration between specialities.

Data on the number of obstetric patients requiring critical care and the makeup of those patients (antenatal/postnatal, levels of care and organ support) have been included and published since 2020 — providing invaluable information through the COVID pandemic about usage of critical care beds and interventions in this population.

SICSAG data has been included in several publications, including the Scottish element of the NMPA MCC sprint audit and Severe maternal morbidity in Scotland (Anaesthesia, 2022), promoting academic collaboration between the maternity and critical care communities.

The network also enabled a coordinated obstetric anaesthetic response to the COVID pandemic across Scottish maternity units, facilitating seamless communication between the obstetric and critical care communities.

Key milestones

A decade of progress

2013

First Unit Joins SICSAG Audit

Ninewells Hospital, Tayside becomes the first Scottish maternity unit to participate in the SICSAG critical care audit.

2014

SMaCC Network Founded

A group of obstetric anaesthetists come together to develop the Scottish Obstetric HDU education course for midwives under SMMDP/NHS Education Scotland.

2015

First SICSAG Data Entry Published

Obstetric data appears for the first time in the SICSAG annual report.

2019

Dedicated Obstetric Section & 7th Unit Joins

Ayrshire Maternity Unit joins the audit. The SICSAG report now features a dedicated obstetric section, including baby-with-mum and extra care indicators.

2020

Critical Care Data Published & COVID Response

Full data on obstetric patients requiring critical care published. The network facilitates a coordinated obstetric anaesthetic response to the COVID pandemic across Scottish units.

2022

Academic Publication: Anaesthesia

Severe maternal morbidity in Scotland published in Anaesthesia, featuring Scottish SICSAG data and promoting collaboration between maternity and critical care communities.
