For Patients & Families

Information and Support

If you or someone you love has experienced critical illness during or after pregnancy, these resources are here to help. Recovery from a critical illness can be a long and difficult journey — you are not alone.

Critical illness around the time of pregnancy and birth is rare, but when it happens it can be frightening and overwhelming — for patients and families alike. The SMaCC Network is focused on improving the care you receive, and we want to make sure you know where to turn for support during recovery.

The resources below have been developed by patients, families, researchers, and healthcare professionals to help guide you through intensive care, recovery, and life afterwards.

Patient Resources

Helpful Resources

These organisations provide information and support for patients and families who have experienced critical illness.

Intensive Care Support

ICUsteps

ICUsteps is the UK's intensive care patient support charity, dedicated to making sure that surviving critical illness is just the beginning of recovery — not the end of support. They provide clear, accessible information for patients and relatives at every stage, from what to expect in the ICU to life after discharge.

  • Guides to what intensive care is and what to expect
  • Information for relatives visiting a loved one in ICU
  • Advice on recovery — physical, psychological, and emotional
  • Glossary of ICU terms to help you understand what's happening
  • Support groups for patients and families across the UK
Visit ICUsteps → icusteps.org
Recovery Resource

Critical Care Recovery

Developed by researchers, patients, family members, and healthcare staff, Critical Care Recovery is an evidence-based website to help patients navigate their recovery from intensive care. It is based on over 120 interviews with former ICU patients and family members at different stages of recovery, and includes a dedicated section for patients affected by COVID-19.

  • Personalised information based on your recovery stage
  • Honest accounts from former ICU patients and their families
  • Guidance on common physical and psychological effects after ICU
  • Read-aloud and translation features for accessibility
  • Separate COVID-19 ICU recovery resource available
Visit the Site → criticalcarerecovery.com

Share Your Experience

Tell Us About Your Care

Your feedback matters. CareOpinion is an independent, not-for-profit platform where you can share your experience of NHS care — good or bad. Stories are read by the teams involved and can directly lead to improvements in care for future patients.

You can share your experience anonymously, and your story will be seen by the care teams and used to improve maternity and critical care services across Scotland.

Share Your Story → careopinion.org.uk
