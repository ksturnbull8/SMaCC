Related Organisations

SMaCC works alongside a number of organisations across obstetric anaesthesia and critical care. Here you'll find links to closely related societies — so you can hop directly to their websites rather than us keeping track of everything on your behalf.

Obstetric Anaesthesia

Obstetric Anaesthetists' Association

OAA

The OAA is the UK's professional society for anaesthetists with a specialist interest in obstetric anaesthesia. It supports its members through education, training, and the development of clinical standards, and represents the specialty at a national and international level.

The OAA runs an annual scientific meeting, produces guidelines and resources for clinicians, and hosts LabourPains — a patient-facing resource on pain relief in labour. SMaCC members regularly attend and present at OAA events, and the two organisations share an interest in improving the safety and quality of care for pregnant women.

Visit OAA oaa-anaes.ac.uk
Critical Care

Scottish Intensive Care Society

SICS

SICS is the professional society supporting critical care clinicians across Scotland. It oversees the Scottish Intensive Care Society Audit Group (SICSAG), which collects and publishes data from Scotland's critical care and high dependency units — including, since 2015, obstetric HDU data gathered in partnership with SMaCC.

SICS has an active education programme, supports the wellbeing of critical care staff, and provides resources for patients and families. The SICSAG annual report is a key publication for maternal critical care audit in Scotland, and SMaCC's ongoing collaboration with SICSAG is central to our data collection work.

Visit SICS scottishintensivecare.org.uk
Obstetric Anaesthesia

West of Scotland Obstetric Anaesthetists

WOSOA

WOSOA is the regional obstetric anaesthesia society for the West of Scotland, bringing together consultants and trainees with an interest in the specialty. It runs regular meetings and events, supports trainee education through its abstract and poster competitions, and offers the WOSOA ART Award for research and training.

WOSOA also hosts an Obstetric Anaesthesia Specialist Interest Area (SIA) for trainees, providing resources and guidance for those pursuing a subspecialty interest. Their events complement SMaCC's national programme and provide an important regional forum for shared learning.

Visit WOSOA wosoa.org.uk
